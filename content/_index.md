## The Issue

A beautiful restoration but an incomplete plan, with real impacts on our neighborhood right now.

In Fall 2025, the Our Lady of Lourdes Church on Napoleon Avenue was transformed into The Josephine, an event space for weddings, galas, and non-profit ticketed events. Many in the neighborhood were happy to see a historic building brought back to life after being vacant since Hurricane Katrina.

But the restoration went over budget [^1], which caused a need for a new business plan.  And now the developers want the neighborhood to absorb impacts associated with that budget shortfall. 

They're asking the city to rezone The Josephine to allow [^2]:

* ticketed events for up to 800 people,   
* 10pm on weeknights, and midnight on weekends  
* 13 events/quarter (roughly 1/week)

The City Planning Commission recommended the City Council deny this application to rezone; however, on May 21st, Council Member Harris made a motion to approve with provisos.  The motion and provisos can be read [here](https://cityofno.legistar.com/View.ashx?M=F&ID=15488805&GUID=274CAE66-A1BA-40A5-B1B3-01B0313D356A). 

Our neighbors are concerned about this rapid development without sufficient planning and how it will impact our quality of life, our investments in our homes and businesses, and the culture of our historic New Orleans neighborhood. In particular:

* Flawed and Deceptive Community Engagement Process  
* There are existing parking/traffic problems, which would worsen with an expanded venue.  
  * Zero designated parking spaces at 2400 Napoleon Avenue.  
  * The 56 off-site spots secured by the venue are located four blocks away.  
    * Attendees are not likely to use this.  
  * Vendor parking and unloading.  
  * Employee parking.  
  * Uber/Lyft parking  
  * Reckless / Drunk driving.  
    * Neighbors have experienced drunken driving from venue attendees and side-view mirrors being swiped by delivery vans.  
* Residents have reported blocked driveways, trash, and loud yelling after events.  
* No benefits to the local neighborhood


To date, there has not been a traffic or parking study conducted to understand if our neighborhood can absorb the impacts of this type of development in this area.  And neighbors are all getting a taste of how challenging a venue of this scale would be for our quality of life \- through the problems we encounter already.

What we are saying: Fix the existing problems, don’t expand them.

**Our request to City Council** \- Deny this application for an ordinance change that would allow live ticketed events in addition to the existing use (private events and non-profit ticketed events). 

**Our request to the developers** \- Work with us on a Good Neighbor Agreement to address the existing issues and to develop a plan that fully addresses traffic, safety, and crowds \- before expanding operations.  Read more about our proposal to The Josephine under Community Proposal.

[^1]: This information was shared by Steve Pettus in a neighborhood meeting with the developers on March 9th.*

[^2]: Neighbors may recall seeing signs in the neutral ground in early March claiming 1350 people until 4am and fear of raves.  No one in this campaign created those signs.  However, those signs did highlight the potential for the space at that time \- 1,341 is the maximum allowed in this space according to fire code.  4am on the weekends is the standard time that live music venues are allowed to host music in the city. Provisos introduced by CM Harris on May 21st listed 800 people until 12pm on weekends.
